# Subatomik Backlog

**Status:** Active Vision & Development  
**Last Updated:** May 16, 2026

---

## Vision

Subatomik as **“Punk Disney / Anti-Apple Apple”** — reclaiming the magic, wonder, and intentionality of early personal computing and thoughtful design, executed with feral, open-source, privacy-first, and DIY energy.

The goal is to build tools and experiences that feel like **digital sovereignty** rather than renting space in someone else’s platform.

---

## Core Philosophy

- Return to **personal home bases** on the internet (evolved from early blogs/homepages).
- High-agency, privacy-first, local-first computing.
- Hybrid analog + digital tools with physical context switching.
- Anti-bloat, anti-surveillance, anti-corporate.
- DIY ethos with soul (inspired by old independent music stores).
- AI + spatial interfaces as a path beyond bloated Web 2.0 tools (Notion, Google, etc.).

---

## Hardware / Cyber Deck Vision

**Core Idea:** A modular, physical, high-agency personal computing rig as the primary interface for technology and the internet.

### Key Concepts
- **Modular Pedal/Box System**: Physical modules you plug in and out (like guitar pedals). One main screen acts as the “amplifier.”
- **Physical Context Switching**: Turning boxes on/off changes your computing environment.
- **DIY + Tactile Aesthetic**: Old music store on Carmine St. vibe — hand-built, repairable, slightly gritty, opinionated gear.
- **Hybrid Analog + Digital**: Physical knobs, switches, and modules combined with digital tools.
- **Maximum Privacy + Minimum Friction**.

### Proposed Modules
- **Grok Pedal**: Metal casing with a large knob that ranges from “Zero” (clean/minimal) to “Feral Bro” (maximum raw/chaotic output).

- [ ] Evaluate base OS options (OpenBSD, FreeBSD, Void Linux, etc.)

## Website as Digital Theme Park

**Core Idea:** The Subatomik website should feel like an immersive, spatial theme park rather than a traditional flat website.

**Influences**: Disney (wonder & immersion), Banksy (punk edge), The Residents (weird/avant-garde), David Lynch (surreal/dreamlike).

### Proposed Areas
- **The Main Gate** — Homepage / Welcome
- **The Library of the Damned** — Bookstore (core experience)
- **The Feral Arcade** — Merch & Swag
- **The Context Pilot Pavilion** — Hardware / Custom Mods
- **The Sideshow** — Blog, Press, Manifesto
- **The Midnight Diner** — Community / Discord
- **The Gift Shop (Exit)** — Library Card signup + final push

### Notable Element
- **The Black Book**: A chained, mysterious book deep in the library with its own lore and warnings.

## Philosophy & Strategic Direction

- The future belongs to people with deliberate, personal, high-agency setups (your gear + modular cyber deck).
- Spatial and meta-level interfaces + AI can replace bloated Web 2.0 tools.
- Subatomik-style spaces could become personal “home bases” on the internet again.
- Strong emphasis on **DIY ethos**, physical tactility, and cultural artifacts over polished corporate products.
